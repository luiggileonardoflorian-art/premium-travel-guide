New York City
Mortgage Strategy Spotlight

New York City presents one of the most complex mortgage environments in the country. Between co-op board approval requirements, the Mansion Tax threshold at $1M, SALT deduction caps, and the city's unique dual income tax structure, borrowers face layered financial decisions that national lenders rarely understand at the local level.

Jumbo loan thresholds, portfolio lending from private banks, and the interplay between NYC's transfer taxes and federal mortgage interest deductions create optimization windows that sophisticated borrowers actively use to reduce total cost of ownership.

Key Consideration

NYC's Mansion Tax creates pricing inflection points at $1M, $2M, $3M, $5M, $10M, $15M, $20M, and $25M. Sophisticated buyers structure purchase prices strategically around these thresholds to reduce transfer tax liability.
