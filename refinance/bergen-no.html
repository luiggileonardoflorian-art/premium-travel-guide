Bergen is Norway’s second‑largest city and the historic centre of the country’s maritime and oil industries. Surrounded by seven mountains and the fjords, it is home to Equinor’s headquarters, a thriving seafood sector, and a major university. The Norwegian mortgage market – with its 85% LTV limit, 5x income rule, and fixed‑rate preference – applies equally here. Prime residential areas like Fjellsiden, Nordnes, and Sandviken offer spectacular views and command premium prices.

Why Bergen’s Mortgage Market is Anchored by Oil, Seafood and Maritime Industry

Bergen’s economy is built on its role as Norway’s energy capital (Equinor headquarters), a global seafood export hub, and a centuries‑old maritime centre. This creates a borrower base with high incomes, substantial bonuses, and strong job security. Lenders apply the national rules – 85% maximum LTV, total debt limited to five times gross income, and stress‑testing at elevated rates. The city’s unique topography, with its seven mountains and fjord‑side location, means that many properties enjoy spectacular views, which can command a premium in valuations.

Oil & Gas / Energy

Equinor’s headquarters are in Bergen, along with numerous suppliers. Engineers, geologists and executives receive high base salaries and bonuses. Lenders average variable income over two to three years. Permanent contracts are standard, making mortgage approval straightforward.

Seafood & Maritime

Bergen is the world capital of seafood exports. Companies like Mowi and Lerøy Seafood employ thousands. Shipowners, brokers, and maritime engineers also form a key part of the economy. Income can include dividends and variable compensation.

Academia & Public Sector

The University of Bergen and Haukeland University Hospital are major employers. Public sector workers enjoy stable, pensionable incomes that lenders view favourably.

“Bergen’s mortgage market runs on the twin engines of oil and fish – both highly cyclical, but with deep‑pocketed employers and a resilient local economy.”

Bergen Luxury Enclaves & Financing Nuances

  • Fjellsiden (Mountainside): NOK 6M–12M, historic wooden houses on the slopes of Mount Fløyen. Panoramic city and fjord views. High demand, limited supply. Lenders may require 30‑40% deposits.
  • Nordnes / Verftet: NOK 5M–10M, waterfront peninsula with a mix of modern apartments and traditional houses. Popular with professionals and families.
  • Sandviken / Kalfaret: NOK 6M–15M, elegant 19th‑century neighbourhoods close to the city centre. Large villas with gardens. Often require substantial equity.

Norwegian Mortgage Rules & Affordability

Norwegian regulators require that total debt does not exceed five times gross annual income. This applies to all borrowers. Lenders also stress‑test your ability to withstand a 5‑6% interest rate. The maximum loan‑to‑value ratio is 85%; any amount above 60% must be amortised. Interest payments are tax‑deductible at 22% (2026 rate). Bergen’s strong labour market and high incomes make it easier for qualified buyers to meet these criteria.

Strategic insight: For oil and seafood executives with large bonuses, maintain a stable base salary and document bonus history to maximise borrowing capacity.

Document Stamp Duty & Other Costs

When buying a property, you pay a document stamp duty (dokumentavgift) of 2.5% of the purchase price for a title deed (for houses and freehold apartments). For condominiums (borettslag), there is no stamp duty, but a small registration fee (approx. NOK 1,000). Mortgage arrangement fees are typically low. Annual property tax is set by the municipality (approx. 2‑4 ‰ of the property value in Bergen).

Bergen Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum down payment for a property in Bergen?
You need at least 15% of the purchase price as a down payment. The maximum loan‑to‑value ratio is 85%. For luxury properties, banks may require 20‑30% down.
How are Equinor bonuses treated for mortgage applications?
Lenders typically average bonus income over the last two to three years. A stable bonus history can be fully included. A letter from your employer confirming expected bonus levels is helpful. For senior executives, guaranteed bonuses may be counted as base income.
What is the 5x income rule?
Norwegian regulators require that your total debt (including the mortgage) does not exceed five times your gross annual income. This is a strict guideline that lenders must follow. It limits borrowing capacity, especially in high‑price areas like Bergen.
What are the costs of buying a home in Bergen?
For a house (freehold), you pay document stamp duty of 2.5% of the purchase price. For a condominium (borettslag), there is no stamp duty, only a small registration fee (approx. NOK 1,000). Mortgage arrangement fees are usually low. Annual property tax in Bergen is around 2‑4 ‰ of the property value.
