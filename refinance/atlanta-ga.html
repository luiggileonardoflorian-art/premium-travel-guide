Why Atlanta’s Mortgage Market Is Different

Atlanta’s explosive growth is driven by corporate relocations, a booming film industry (“Yollywood”), and an influx of residents from California, New York, and Illinois. This migration creates unique underwriting needs: porting equity, qualifying with non‑W2 income (residuals, carried interest), and navigating Georgia’s homestead exemption. Lenders here are adept at handling complex cross‑state tax situations and jumbo loans for second‑home buyers in the North Georgia mountains.

The Migration Factor

Over 100,000 people moved to Georgia in 2024, many from high‑tax states. Lenders now offer “equity transfer” programs that use the sale of a prior home (often in CA or NY) to qualify for a new jumbo loan before the old home closes. Bridge loans and asset‑based underwriting are common for relocating executives.

Film & Creative Income

Georgia’s film industry generated $4.4B in 2024. Actors, directors, and crew often have irregular, project‑based income. Lenders use two‑year averages of residuals, contracts, and bank statements. “Bank statement only” loans are popular for self‑employed creatives.

Fortune 500 Executive Relo

With 16 Fortune 500 headquarters, Atlanta sees a steady stream of C‑suite relocations. These buyers often need super‑jumbo loans ($2.5M+) and benefit from relationship pricing through private banks. RSUs from former employers (e.g., a Calif. tech giant) can be used as pledged assets.

“Atlanta is where old‑line Southern wealth meets new‑economy migration. The lender who understands both wins the deal.”

Atlanta Luxury Enclaves & Financing Nuances

  • Buckhead: $2M–$15M estates; super‑jumbo ARMs, portfolio lenders; many gated communities require additional HOA underwriting.
  • Midtown: $700k–$3M luxury condos; warrantable vs. non‑warrantable buildings; many new high‑rises with special assessment risks.
  • Alpharetta / Johns Creek: $800k–$2.5M family homes; strong demand from tech execs; jumbo conforming up to $1.5M, then portfolio.
  • Lake Burton / North Georgia: $1.5M–$5M vacation homes; second‑home jumbo rates, often DSCR for rental income.

Georgia Homestead Exemption

Georgia offers a $10,000 homestead exemption off the assessed value for primary residences, plus additional county‑specific exemptions (Fulton County up to $30k for seniors). This lowers the effective tax rate to ~0.87% vs. a national 1.1%. For financing, lenders factor in the lower tax burden, boosting DTI capacity.

Strategic insight: Buyers relocating from high‑tax states (CA, NJ) often see their property tax bill cut in half. This can free up cash flow for a larger mortgage or investment.

No State Income Tax on Retirement

Georgia exempts Social Security and most retirement income from state tax. This attracts retirees and second‑home buyers. For mortgage qualifying, these exemptions increase net income, making it easier to qualify for jumbo loans.

Atlanta Frequently Asked Questions

I’m relocating from California for a job at Coca‑Cola. Can I use my RSUs to qualify?
Yes. Lenders will average 24 months of RSU vesting (W‑2 or Schedule D). If you have unvested RSUs, some private banks offer “RSU forward” programs. A pledged asset mortgage using your brokerage account can also provide liquidity without selling.
How does the homestead exemption affect my mortgage qualification?
Lenders will use the actual property tax amount after exemption when calculating your debt‑to‑income ratio. If you file for homestead, your tax bill drops ~$1,000‑$2,000/year, slightly increasing your borrowing capacity.
Can I get a jumbo loan with income from film residuals?
Absolutely. Non‑QM lenders offer bank statement loans that average 12‑24 months of deposits. Residual income can be documented via contracts and royalty statements. Many Atlanta lenders specialize in entertainment industry financing.
