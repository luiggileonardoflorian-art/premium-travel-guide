Why Boston’s Mortgage Market Operates Differently

The confluence of academic medicine, biotechnology, and asset management creates a borrower base with unique income streams: physician contracts, biotech equity, and carried interest. Meanwhile, Boston’s historic housing stock and limited developable land keep inventory tight and prices elevated. Lenders here must navigate physician loan programs, RSU‑heavy underwriting, and the nuances of Massachusetts property tax law (Proposition 2½).

Physician & Medical Staff Lending

With Mass General, Brigham, Boston Children’s, and numerous teaching hospitals, Boston has one of the highest concentrations of physicians in the U.S. Many lenders offer doctor loans with 0% down, no PMI, and flexible debt‑to‑income ratios that consider future earning potential. These programs are essential for early‑career attendings and fellows.

Biotech Equity & RSUs

From Kendall Square to the Seaport, biotech executives and scientists often hold concentrated equity in publicly traded or pre‑IPO companies. Lenders experienced in the sector will underwrite vested RSUs as income, and some private banks offer pledged‑asset mortgages against stock in companies like Moderna, Biogen, or Vertex.

Historic Properties & Condo Specialists

Beacon Hill brownstones and Back Bay row houses often come with historic designations, requiring specialized underwriting for renovation escrows. Meanwhile, luxury high‑rises in the Seaport have condo regime warranties that affect financing. Portfolio lenders who understand Boston’s unique housing stock are essential.

“In Boston, the most creditworthy borrower might be a surgeon with a 800 credit score but three years of self‑employment — physician loans bridge that gap.”

Boston Luxury Enclaves & Financing Nuances

  • Back Bay / Beacon Hill: $2M–$12M historic homes; many with condominium conversion or HOA complexities; jumbo ARMs from relationship banks.
  • Seaport District / Fort Point: $1.5M–$5M luxury condos; many new construction warrantable; some buildings have rental restrictions affecting investment loans.
  • Cambridge (Harvard Sq., Kendall): $1.2M–$4M condos and single‑family; high concentration of biotech and academic buyers; bank statement loans for self‑employed consultants.
  • Brookline / Newton: $1.5M–$6M family homes; top school districts; physician loan demand is high; land value makes up significant portion of appraised value.

Massachusetts Proposition 2½ & Financing

Prop 2½ limits the annual increase in property tax levy to 2.5% (plus new growth). However, upon purchase the property is reassessed to current market value, often resulting in a significant tax jump. For long‑time owners, the tax basis may be far below market, creating a retention benefit. Refinancing does NOT trigger reassessment, but adding an owner (e.g., through transfer) might. Important for estate planning and intergenerational transfers.

Strategic insight: When buying in Boston, factor in the new tax basis (approx 1.2% of purchase price). For inherited property, you may keep the low basis if you qualify for the parent‑child exclusion (subject to cap).

High Incomes, High Student Debt

Many Boston professionals carry substantial student loans. For physicians, lenders may exclude deferred loans from DTI. For others, student loan payments affect debt ratios. Some jumbo lenders offer refinance options that consolidate mortgage and student debt, but proceed with caution.

Boston Frequently Asked Questions

What is a physician loan and how do I qualify in Boston?
Physician loans are specialized mortgages for medical professionals (MDs, DOs, DDS, DPM, etc.) offering 0% down, no PMI, and flexible underwriting. Most require a signed employment contract for attending positions; residents/fellows may qualify with proof of future income. Several Boston‑based lenders offer these, including regional banks and credit unions.
Can I use biotech stock options to qualify for a mortgage?
Yes, vested RSUs are treated as income using a 24‑month average. Unvested options are harder to use, but some private banks will consider them in a pledged‑asset mortgage where you collateralize your brokerage account. For pre‑IPO stock, specialized lenders may offer loans based on a discount to the latest 409A valuation.
How does Proposition 2½ affect my refinance or purchase?
When you buy a home, it is reassessed to the purchase price, so property taxes will reflect that new value. Refinancing does not trigger reassessment. However, if you add or remove an owner (except for certain exemptions), the property may be reassessed. Always consult a tax professional before making ownership changes.
