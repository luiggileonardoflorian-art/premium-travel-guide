Why Houston’s Mortgage Market Differs

Houston’s lack of personal income tax, heavy reliance on energy and medical sectors, and vulnerability to flooding create a specialized lending environment. Lenders evaluate 1099 income from geologists, royalties from mineral rights, and flood insurance requirements alongside traditional metrics.

Energy Sector & 1099 Income

Houston is home to thousands of energy consultants, geologists, and engineers who work as independent contractors. Lenders familiar with the sector offer bank statement loans and asset‑based underwriting that smooth out cyclical bonus income. Royalty income from mineral rights can be used to qualify when documented with two years of tax returns.

Texas Medical Center & Physician Loans

The world’s largest medical complex employs thousands of physicians, researchers, and specialists. Many lenders offer physician mortgage loans with 0% down, no PMI, and flexible underwriting for high debt‑to‑income ratios. Contracts, not tax returns, can qualify newly hired attendings.

Flood Zones & Insurance Nuances

Much of Harris County lies in a floodplain. Lenders require flood insurance for properties in Special Flood Hazard Areas. Post‑Harvey, many lenders also scrutinize elevation certificates and may require additional reserves. Some jumbo lenders offer portfolio products that self‑insure against flood risk.

“In Houston, a doctor’s first contract or an oil executive’s bonus history can be stronger than a traditional W‑2. The right lender knows how to underwrite real cash flow.”

Houston Luxury Enclaves & Lending Nuances

  • River Oaks / Uptown: $2M–$15M estates; super‑jumbo ARMs, private bank relationship pricing; many lots are high‑value with low structures — land‑value considerations.
  • Memorial / Hunters Creek: $1.5M–$6M; established neighborhoods, frequent out‑of‑state corporate relocations — need for rate locks and remote closing expertise.
  • The Woodlands / Sugar Land: $700k–$2.5M; master‑planned communities with MUD taxes — lenders must factor in additional special district assessments.
  • Galveston Bay / Waterfront: $1M–$5M; second homes and investment properties — DSCR loans for rentals, additional wind and flood insurance requirements.

Texas Property Tax & Homestead Exemption

Texas has no state income tax, but property taxes are among the highest in the U.S. The average effective rate in Harris County is ~1.8%. A homestead exemption reduces taxable value by $40,000 (or more for seniors/disabled). School district taxes are also capped. Unlike Prop 13, there is no annual increase limit; reappraisals occur yearly based on market value. This can create tax volatility, which lenders account for in qualifying.

Strategic insight: When purchasing in Houston, factor in potential property tax increases. Lenders often use a 2–3% annual appreciation assumption; you may want to escrow aggressively. Also, homestead exemptions can be filed retroactively for up to two years.

No State Income Tax – DTI Impact

Because Texas residents pay no state income tax, their after‑tax income is higher than in high‑tax states. For a household earning $500,000, this can mean an extra $30,000+ in annual cash flow. Lenders may allow a higher DTI cap or use the additional cash flow to qualify for a larger loan. However, the high property tax partially offsets this benefit.

Houston Frequently Asked Questions

How do I qualify with oil & gas 1099 income?
Lenders typically require two years of tax returns showing consistent or growing income. If you have significant fluctuations, a bank statement program (12–24 months of deposits) may be a better fit. Some portfolio lenders also consider contracts and invoices for large projects.
What is the homestead exemption and how does it affect my mortgage?
The Texas homestead exemption reduces your home’s taxable value for school district taxes by at least $40,000. It lowers your property tax bill, which improves your debt‑to‑income ratio. You must apply with the county appraisal district; lenders may require proof of exemption to use the lower tax amount in underwriting.
Do I need flood insurance in Houston?
If your property lies in a FEMA Special Flood Hazard Area, flood insurance is mandatory for federally regulated loans. Even outside high‑risk zones, many lenders recommend it. Premiums can be significant; factor them into your total housing cost. Some jumbo lenders offer in‑house flood coverage alternatives.
