Military, Medicine & the Texas Boom

San Antonio’s economy is anchored by four military installations, a world‑class health sector, and a surging tech corridor. Lenders here are accustomed to VA borrowers, physician mortgages, and first‑time homebuyer programs that reflect the city’s demographic diversity.

VA Lending Hub

Joint Base San Antonio (Fort Sam Houston, Lackland, Randolph) makes San Antonio one of the largest VA loan markets in the country. Local lenders have dedicated VA specialists who understand Certificate of Eligibility timelines, funding fee exemptions for disabled veterans, and the nuances of refinancing under the Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan (IRRRL).

Physician & Medical Loans

With UT Health San Antonio and multiple major hospital systems (Methodist, Baptist, Christus), the city attracts hundreds of new doctors, residents, and nurses annually. Many lenders offer physician mortgage programs with zero down payment, no PMI, and flexible underwriting for high debt‑to‑income ratios — ideal for medical professionals early in their careers.

First‑Time Buyer Programs

San Antonio’s affordability (median home price well below national average) makes it a magnet for first‑time buyers. The city offers down payment assistance grants, Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation (TSAHC) programs, and FHA/ conventional loans with competitive terms. Local credit unions and community banks often have specialized products for low‑ to moderate‑income households.

“San Antonio’s lending ecosystem is built on stability — military paychecks, healthcare employment, and a cost of living that allows families to build equity faster than in almost any other major Texas metro.”

The Texas Tax Advantage

No state income tax means more disposable income for mortgage payments. However, Texas has among the highest effective property tax rates in the U.S. (averaging 1.6–2.0% of home value). For San Antonio homeowners, this trade‑off is critical: the absence of income tax frees up cash flow, but property tax must be factored into monthly housing costs.

  • 0% state income tax — boosts qualifying income for conventional loans
  • Homestead exemption reduces taxable value by $40,000+ in Bexar County
  • Over‑65 or disabled exemptions further lower property tax burden

Neighborhood Financing Profiles

San Antonio’s neighborhoods range from historic districts to master‑planned communities, each with distinct lending considerations.

  • King William / Southtown: Historic designation may require additional documentation for renovations; high walkability premium.
  • Alamo Heights / Terrell Hills: Strong resale, competitive jumbo market; many buyers use conventional 30‑year fixed.
  • Far West / Helotes: New construction; must verify builder incentives and temporary buydowns.
  • Brooks / South Side: Revitalization area; FHA 203(k) and renovation loans are common.

San Antonio’s Core Loan Products

Three financing categories that dominate the Alamo City market. Understanding each helps match your profile to the right lender.

VA Loans

VA & Military Home Financing

For active‑duty service members, veterans, and eligible surviving spouses, VA loans offer zero down payment, no PMI, and competitive rates. San Antonio lenders process more VA loans than almost any other city; many have streamlined underwriting for BAH (Basic Allowance for Housing) and combat pay.

VA Key Features

  • 100% financing — no down payment required
  • Funding fee can be rolled into loan; waived for disabled veterans
  • No PMI (private mortgage insurance) — lower monthly payment
  • IRRRL streamline refinance available

Texas Tax Structure & Wealth Planning

No state income tax is a powerful wealth‑building tool, but property taxes and the state’s unique legal protections matter just as much.

Property Tax Mitigation

Texas has the 6th highest property tax rate in the U.S., but savvy homeowners use several tools to manage the burden:

Homestead Exemption

  • $40,000 off appraised value for school taxes (Bexar Co.)
  • Additional $3,000 for non‑school district taxes
  • Over‑65 or disabled: additional $10,000 exemption

Protest & Ceilings

  • Annual appraisal protest window (May 15 or 30 days)
  • 10% appraisal cap per year for homesteads
  • Use comps to dispute valuation

Insight: File your homestead exemption as soon as you close — it can save $1,000+ annually.

Texas Homestead Protection

Under the Texas Constitution, a homestead is protected from forced sale by most judgment creditors (with exceptions for purchase money, taxes, and home equity loans). This unlimited protection applies regardless of home value — a critical asset protection feature for physicians, business owners, and professionals with liability exposure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Real questions from San Antonio homebuyers — educational overviews only.

I’m active duty military with orders to JBSA. Can I use my VA loan benefit immediately?

Yes, you can use your VA loan benefit immediately if you have a valid Certificate of Eligibility (COE) and meet service requirements. Many San Antonio lenders are experienced with military buyers and can often close in 30 days or less. Important: If you’re a first‑time user, the VA funding fee (2.15% for regular military, 0% if you receive VA disability) can be rolled into the loan. Lenders here also understand BAH (Basic Allowance for Housing) and treat it as effective income.

What down payment assistance is available for first‑time buyers in San Antonio?

Several programs exist: Bexar County DPA offers up to $10,000 in a 0% forgivable loan (forgiven after 5 years). TSAHC’s “Home Sweet Texas” provides 30‑year fixed loans with 3–5% down and grants up to 5% of the loan amount for down payment/closing costs. SETH (San Antonio Education & Training for Homeownership) offers classes and connection to DPA funds. Income and purchase price limits apply — most are geared toward first‑time buyers (or those who haven’t owned in 3 years).

How do property taxes affect my monthly payment in San Antonio compared to other Texas cities?

Bexar County’s effective tax rate (around 1.9%) is slightly below the Texas average (~1.95%) but higher than some suburban counties. On a $335,000 home, annual property taxes are roughly $6,365, or $530/month. That’s often higher than the national average property tax payment, but it’s offset by no state income tax. Always use a mortgage calculator that includes property taxes to get your true PITI payment.

Are there specialized loans for medical professionals in San Antonio?

Yes. Several national and regional lenders offer physician mortgage loans to doctors, dentists, and sometimes nurses. Features: 0–5% down, no PMI, and they exclude student loan debt from DTI calculations (or use a very favorable calculation). UT Health San Antonio and the city’s major hospitals recruit hundreds of new physicians each year, so local lenders are very familiar with these products. You’ll typically need a signed employment contract and proof of residency completion.
