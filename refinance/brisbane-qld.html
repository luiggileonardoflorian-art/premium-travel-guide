Brisbane is Australia’s rising sunbelt capital, fuelled by the resources sector, major healthcare and education institutions, and a booming construction industry. The mortgage market here serves mining executives on fly‑in‑fly‑out (FIFO) arrangements, healthcare professionals, academics, and a growing number of self‑employed tradies. Queensland’s stamp duty and land tax rules, combined with the city’s accelerating property market, demand strategic planning.

Why Brisbane’s Mortgage Market is Australia’s Fastest‑Growing

Brisbane’s economy is powered by resources (coal, gas, mining services), major universities, and a sprawling public health sector. The city’s relatively affordable property prices (compared to Sydney/Melbourne) attract interstate investors and first‑home buyers. Lenders here are accustomed to FIFO income with high base salaries and allowances, as well as self‑employed tradies and contractors. Queensland’s stamp duty concessions for first‑home buyers and foreign surcharges shape the market.

FIFO & Mining Income

Brisbane is the headquarters for many mining companies, and thousands of workers commute to sites across Queensland. Lenders typically require a history of continuous employment and may average allowances and bonuses over 2‑3 years. Some banks have specialist teams for resources sector employees, accepting letters of guarantee for ongoing work.

Healthcare & Education

Queensland Health and the state’s universities are major employers. Doctors, nurses, and academics enjoy stable income. Overtime for healthcare workers is common; lenders average it over 3‑6 months. Academics on research grants need a history of renewals. Some lenders offer professional packages for medical practitioners.

Self‑Employed & Tradies

Brisbane’s construction boom means many self‑employed tradies seek mortgages. Lenders require 2‑3 years of tax returns. “Low doc” loans (higher rates) are available with one year’s accounts or BAS statements. Some specialist lenders use business cash flow rather than just net profit.

“Brisbane’s property market is heating up, but lenders remain cautious with FIFO and self‑employed income – solid documentation is key.”

Brisbane Luxury Enclaves & Financing Nuances

  • Ascot / Hamilton: $1.8M–$5M+, prestige riverfront homes. High demand, often selling prior to auction. Jumbo loans require strong serviceability and may need 30% deposits.
  • New Farm / Teneriffe: $1.5M–$4M, character homes and luxury apartments close to the city. Popular with professionals – pre‑approval essential for auctions.
  • St Lucia / Toowong: $1.2M–$2.5M, close to University of Queensland. High demand from academics and investors. Flood risk in some pockets – insurance required.

Stamp Duty & First Home Buyer Benefits

In Queensland, stamp duty for a $850,000 home is approximately $25,000. First‑time buyers may be eligible for a full concession on properties up to $500,000, and a tapered concession up to $550,000. The First Home Owner Grant (FHOG) offers $15,000 for new builds (house and land packages) up to $750,000. For foreign buyers, a surcharge of 7% applies on top of standard rates, making investment significantly more expensive.

Strategic insight: First‑time buyers should target new builds under $750k to access the FHOG.

Land Tax for Investors

Investors in Queensland pay land tax on investment properties above the threshold ($600,000 for individuals, $350,000 for companies/trusts in 2026). Rates are progressive, up to 1.5% for high‑value holdings. Owner‑occupiers are exempt. This annual cost must be factored into cash flow. Some investors hold properties in trusts to manage land tax exposure, but trust structures may affect borrowing capacity.

Brisbane Frequently Asked Questions

How do lenders treat FIFO (fly‑in‑fly‑out) income for miners?
Most lenders accept FIFO income with a history of continuous employment. They will average allowances and bonuses over 2‑3 years. A letter from your employer confirming ongoing work can help. Some banks have specialist teams for resources sector employees and may offer slightly higher borrowing multiples.
What is the First Home Owner Grant in Queensland?
The FHOG in Queensland is $15,000 for first‑time buyers purchasing or building a new home valued up to $750,000. It is not available for established homes. You must be an Australian citizen or permanent resident, and have never owned a property before. The grant helps with the deposit but is paid at settlement.
Is Brisbane a good place for property investment?
Yes, Brisbane offers strong rental yields (around 4%) and solid capital growth due to interstate migration and infrastructure spending. However, investors must factor in land tax (above $600,000 threshold) and stamp duty costs. Foreign investors face a 7% surcharge. Limited company or trust ownership can be tax‑efficient but may affect borrowing.
What documents do I need if I'm self‑employed in Brisbane?
Typically, you’ll need 2‑3 years of tax returns and Notice of Assessments. If you’re a sole trader, business activity statements (BAS) may also be considered. For “low doc” loans (with one year’s accounts), you’ll need an accountant’s declaration and a higher deposit (often 20%+). Some lenders accept business bank statements as proof of cash flow.
