Why Adelaide’s Mortgage Market is Australia’s Most Affordable Capital

Adelaide offers the most affordable housing among Australian mainland capitals, attracting first‑home buyers and investors alike. The economy is driven by defense (submarine and frigate building), health, and education. This creates a borrower base with stable government and defense contractor income, as well as a growing number of self‑employed professionals. Lenders are accustomed to FIFO defense workers and academics, but affordability remains the key theme.

Defense & Shipbuilding Income

ASC, BAE Systems, and other defense primes employ thousands of engineers, project managers, and tradespeople. Many work on long‑term contracts with allowances. Lenders typically require 2‑3 years of history and may average allowances. Security‑cleared roles are well regarded.

Health & Education

SA Health is the state’s largest employer, with doctors, nurses, and allied health professionals. Overtime and penalty rates are common. Academics from three universities enjoy stable income, though contract researchers need to show renewal history. Some lenders offer professional packages for medical practitioners.

Affordability & First‑Home Buyers

Adelaide’s median house price remains below $700k, making it accessible. First‑home buyers benefit from stamp duty concessions and the First Home Owner Grant (FHOG) for new builds. This drives demand in outer suburbs and new developments.

“Adelaide’s affordability is its superpower – but with defense and health driving incomes, borrowers can still achieve premium lending terms in the city’s best suburbs.”

Adelaide Luxury Enclaves & Financing Nuances

  • Unley / Malvern: $1.2M–$2.5M+, leafy inner‑south suburbs with character homes. Strong demand from professionals. Renovation mortgages available for period properties.
  • North Adelaide: $1M–$3M, prestigious historic homes and new apartments close to the city and university. High demand from academics and executives.
  • Henley Beach / Grange: $1M–$2M, coastal suburbs with strong capital growth. Lenders require valuations to match premium prices.

Stamp Duty & First Home Buyer Benefits

In South Australia, stamp duty for a $680,000 home is approximately $28,000. First‑time buyers may be eligible for a full exemption on properties up to $600,000, and a tapered concession up to $700,000. The First Home Owner Grant (FHOG) offers $15,000 for new builds (house and land packages) up to $575,000. For foreign buyers, a surcharge of 7% applies on top of standard rates.

Strategic insight: First‑time buyers should target properties under $600k to access full stamp duty exemption.

Land Tax for Investors

Investors in South Australia pay land tax on investment properties above the threshold ($450,000 for individuals, $25,000 for companies/trusts in 2026). Rates are progressive, up to 1.5% for high‑value holdings. Owner‑occupiers are exempt. This annual cost must be factored into cash flow. Some investors hold properties in trusts to manage land tax exposure, but trust structures may affect borrowing capacity.

Adelaide Frequently Asked Questions

How do lenders treat defense contractor income in Adelaide?
Lenders typically require 2‑3 years of contract history and may average allowances. A letter from your employer confirming ongoing work is essential. Some banks have specialist teams for defense industry workers, recognising the long‑term nature of major projects like the submarine program.
What is the First Home Owner Grant in South Australia?
The FHOG in SA is $15,000 for first‑time buyers purchasing or building a new home valued up to $575,000. It is not available for established homes. You must be an Australian citizen or permanent resident, and have never owned a property before. The grant helps with the deposit but is paid at settlement.
Is Adelaide a good place for property investment?
Yes, Adelaide offers attractive rental yields (around 4‑5%) and lower entry prices than eastern capitals. Population growth and infrastructure spending are positive. Investors must factor in land tax (above $450,000 threshold) and stamp duty costs. Foreign investors face a 7% surcharge. Limited company or trust ownership can be tax‑efficient but may affect borrowing.
What documents do I need if I'm self‑employed in Adelaide?
Typically, you’ll need 2‑3 years of tax returns and Notice of Assessments. If you’re a sole trader, business activity statements (BAS) may also be considered. For “low doc” loans (with one year’s accounts), you’ll need an accountant’s declaration and a larger deposit (often 20%+). Some lenders accept business bank statements as proof of cash flow.
