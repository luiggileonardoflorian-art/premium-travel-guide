Why San Diego’s Mortgage Market Differs

San Diego blends coastal luxury with a stable biotech/defense economy. Jumbo lending is common, but lenders must navigate California’s property tax quirks (Prop 13) and income from stock options, military housing allowances, and medical professional contracts.

Biotech & Defense Equity

Qualcomm, Illumina, and defense contractors grant RSUs and bonuses. Underwriters familiar with San Diego will average equity income and may offer pledged-asset loans against concentrated positions, avoiding capital gains while financing a Del Mar estate.

Prop 13 & Coastal Inheritance

Many La Jolla and Coronado properties have been held for decades with artificially low tax bases. Refinancing does not trigger reassessment, but adding a spouse or trust may. Strategic use of parent‑child exclusions preserves low taxes across generations.

Military & Medical Income

With Naval Base San Diego and top hospitals, lenders often see BAH (Basic Allowance for Housing) and physician contracts. VA loans are popular, but for jumbo amounts, bank statement or asset-based programs help self-employed specialists.

“In San Diego, the blend of life sciences equity and military stability creates a unique underwriting profile — lenders must understand both RSUs and tax-free allowances.”

Premium Enclaves & Financing Nuances

  • La Jolla / Rancho Santa Fe: $3M–$20M estates; super‑jumbo ARMs, portfolio lenders; many properties with coastal commission restrictions.
  • Del Mar / Solana Beach: $2M–$6M; strong second-home market — vacation home jumbo rates apply.
  • Coronado: $2M–$8M; military-friendly VA jumbo, but condo financing stricter due to HOA rules.
  • Downtown / East Village: $800k–$2.5M condos; warrantability issues for new developments.

Proposition 13 & Financing

Prop 13 caps property tax increases to 2% of the 1976 base (or acquisition value). For long‑held homes, this creates massive tax advantages. Refinancing does NOT trigger reassessment, but cash‑out refis may be limited by LTV. Transferring property between parents and children retains the low basis if a reassessment exclusion is claimed — crucial for estate planning.

Strategic insight: When purchasing a home in San Diego, your property tax basis resets to purchase price (approx 1.2% of sale). But if you inherit a family home with low Prop 13 basis, you may keep that low tax bill.

High Tax, High Service Trade‑off

California’s 13.3% top marginal rate is the nation’s highest. For a $1.5M annual income, the state tax burden is ~$200k. Lenders use after‑tax income; high state tax reduces DTI capacity. Some lenders gross‑up for state tax, but only if properly documented.

San Diego Frequently Asked Questions

I work at Qualcomm/Illumina with RSUs. How do lenders treat them?
Lenders average 24 months of vested RSUs (W‑2 or Schedule D). A two‑year history is required; some private banks also consider unvested RSUs with a discount. Stock price volatility is managed by using a lower 12‑month average if trending up.
Can I get a mortgage using my military BAH and allowances?
Yes. VA loans are ideal, but for jumbo amounts, many lenders will gross up tax‑free BAH and include it as qualifying income. You’ll need LES statements and a stable service record.
Does Prop 13 affect my refinance in Coronado?
No, refinancing does not trigger reassessment. However, cash‑out refis are subject to standard LTV limits. Adding a non‑spouse owner could trigger reassessment. Consult a tax professional.
