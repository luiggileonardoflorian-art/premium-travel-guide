Why Melbourne’s Mortgage Market is Australia’s Most Diverse

Melbourne’s economy is anchored by world‑class universities, major hospitals, and corporate headquarters. This creates a borrower base with stable employment (academics, healthcare professionals) as well as high‑income earners in finance and professional services. The city’s property market is known for its auction culture, requiring pre‑approval and quick decisions. Victoria’s stamp duty surcharges and land tax rules add layers of complexity for investors.

Healthcare & Medical Income

Melbourne is a national healthcare hub with major hospitals and research institutes. Doctors, specialists, and nurses often have complex income including overtime and private practice earnings. Lenders may accept overtime if consistent, and self‑employed doctors can use 2‑3 years of tax returns. Some lenders have dedicated medical professional packages.

Academic & Research Income

The University of Melbourne and Monash employ thousands of academics and researchers. Tenured staff are straightforward; contract researchers with fixed‑term grants need to show a history of renewals. Some lenders accept grant income if it’s been consistent for 2‑3 years and a future grant is confirmed.

Self‑Employed & Small Business

Melbourne’s thriving small business scene means many borrowers are self‑employed. Lenders typically require 2‑3 years of tax returns. “Low doc” loans (with higher rates) are available for those with one year’s accounts or BAS statements. Some lenders use business cash flow rather than just net profit.

Melbourne Luxury Enclaves & Financing Nuances

  • Toorak / South Yarra: $2.5M–$10M+, Melbourne’s prestige postcodes. High demand, often selling prior to auction. Jumbo loans require strong serviceability and may need 30% deposits.
  • Brighton / Hampton: $2M–$5M, bayside suburbs with period homes. Many heritage‑listed properties – renovation loans may require council approvals.
  • Hawthorn / Kew: $1.8M–$4M, leafy eastern suburbs close to top private schools. Strong demand from families, often requiring pre‑approval for auctions.

Stamp Duty & First Home Buyer Benefits

In Victoria, stamp duty for a $1M home is approximately $55,000. First‑time buyers may be eligible for a full exemption on properties up to $600,000 or a concession up to $750,000. For foreign buyers, a surcharge of 8% applies on top of standard rates, making investment significantly more expensive. The First Home Owner Grant (FHOG) offers $10,000 for new builds in regional Victoria, but Melbourne metro is excluded.

Strategic insight: First‑time buyers should target properties under $600k to access full stamp duty exemption.

Land Tax for Investors

Investors in Victoria pay land tax on investment properties above the threshold ($300,000 for individuals, $450,000 for trusts/companies in 2026). Rates are progressive, up to 2.25% for high‑value holdings. Owner‑occupiers are exempt. This annual cost must be factored into cash flow. Some investors hold properties in trusts to manage land tax exposure, but trust structures may affect borrowing capacity.

Melbourne Frequently Asked Questions

How do lenders assess overtime for healthcare professionals?
Most lenders accept overtime if it's regular and likely to continue. They will average the last 3‑6 months of payslips. Some require a 12‑month history. A letter from your employer confirming regular overtime can strengthen your application. For doctors with private practice income, 2‑3 years of tax returns are typically required.
What is the First Home Owner Grant in Victoria?
The FHOG in Victoria is $10,000 for first‑time buyers purchasing or building a new home valued up to $750,000. It is not available for established homes in Melbourne metro. The grant is designed to encourage new construction. You must be an Australian citizen or permanent resident, and have never owned a property before.
Is Melbourne a good place for buy‑to‑let investment?
Yes, Melbourne offers strong rental demand from students, young professionals, and families. However, land tax and stamp duty surcharges (especially for foreign investors) can eat into returns. Investors should consider holding properties in a trust or company structure, but this may affect borrowing capacity. Always run the numbers with a specialist broker and accountant.
What documents do I need if I'm self‑employed in Melbourne?
Typically, you’ll need 2‑3 years of tax returns and Notice of Assessments. If you’re a sole trader, business activity statements (BAS) may also be considered. For “low doc” loans (with one year’s accounts), you’ll need an accountant’s declaration and a higher deposit. Some lenders accept business bank statements as proof of cash flow.
