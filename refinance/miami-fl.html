Wall Street South & the Miami Lending Ecosystem

Miami's ascent is structural, not cyclical. The city has built the institutional depth — private banks, international law firms, multi-currency custody, and specialty lenders — to support financing for the world's most complex borrowers. Understanding the distinct loan product landscape is essential before approaching any Miami lender.

The Brickell Financial Migration

The relocation of Citadel's global headquarters to Brickell crystallized what had been a decade-long undercurrent: Miami is now a genuine global financial district. For high-net-worth borrowers, this concentration of institutional capital means access to portfolio lenders, private banks, and specialty mortgage desks that understand complex income structures — equity compensation, carried interest, and international asset bases — that traditional lenders routinely decline.

The $80B+ LatAm Capital Corridor

Miami is the undisputed U.S. gateway for Latin American private wealth. High-net-worth individuals from Brazil, Colombia, Mexico, Argentina, and Venezuela have historically used Miami as their primary U.S. domicile and financing hub. This inbound capital corridor has created a specialized market for foreign national mortgages, ITIN-based lending, and cross-border trust financing structures that are simply not available at scale anywhere else in the United States.

Asset-Based & Non-QM Lending Hub

Miami's borrower base — disproportionately self-employed founders, international investors, and private equity principals — has driven the growth of one of the most sophisticated non-QM lending markets in the country. Asset depletion lending, DSCR investor loans, bank statement mortgages, and pledged asset financing are not edge-case products here. They are mainstream offerings from Miami's specialty mortgage desks, often with faster timelines and more competitive pricing than equivalent products in other markets.

Miami is no longer a secondary alternative for borrowers who don't qualify elsewhere. It is a primary market with specialized lenders who have built their entire infrastructure around the complex, international, and non-traditional income profiles that define South Florida's borrower base.

The Florida Tax Advantage for Borrowers

Florida imposes no individual income tax, no capital gains tax, and no state estate tax. For a borrower relocating from New York or California, the effective tax savings on a $5M income stream can exceed $500,000 annually — permanently altering the debt-service math on any jumbo mortgage and materially improving qualifying ratios even before underwriting adjustments.

  • 0% state income tax — permanently improves DTI ratios for qualifying
  • No state capital gains tax — affects cash-out refinance and second home strategies
  • Unlimited homestead protection — primary residence cannot be forced-sold by general creditors
  • Save Our Homes cap limits property tax assessment growth to 3% per year for homestead properties

Luxury Neighborhoods & Their Financing Profiles

Each of Miami's premier submarkets presents distinct financing dynamics that affect lender selection, LTV availability, and loan structure.

  • Star Island: Trophy estates $10M–$50M+; most transactions involve portfolio lenders with custom LTV arrangements and non-standard appraisal processes
  • Coral Gables: $2M–$8M range; more conventional jumbo availability; strong HOA and historic district documentation requirements
  • Brickell Condos: High-rise financing subject to condo project approval; foreign national buyers common; may require larger down payments at certain projects
  • Coconut Grove: $3M–$12M waterfront and estate segment; mix of conventional jumbo and portfolio lending; strong appreciation history

Miami's Specialist Loan Product Landscape

Three financing categories that define the Miami market and diverge most significantly from national norms. Understanding each before approaching lenders is essential for high-net-worth and international borrowers.

Jumbo Financing

Ultra-Jumbo & Super-Jumbo Mortgages

Miami's luxury market routinely generates loan amounts of $5M–$30M+ — well beyond standard jumbo thresholds. These "super-jumbo" transactions require portfolio lenders willing to hold loans on their own balance sheets rather than selling them into the secondary market. The underwriting process is fundamentally different: more subjective, relationship-driven, and oriented around total wealth rather than income ratios alone.

Key Underwriting Factors

  • Total net worth vs. loan amount (often target 10:1 or higher)
  • Liquidity post-close: 12–24 months PITI in liquid reserves
  • Source of funds documentation (especially for international buyers)
  • Existing deposits at lending institution improve terms significantly

Foreign National

Foreign National Financing

Miami is one of the few U.S. markets where foreign national mortgages — loans to non-U.S. citizens purchasing U.S. real estate — are a mainstream, well-developed product category. Lenders here have established programs, legal infrastructure, and international wire transfer systems specifically designed for buyers from Latin America, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia who wish to purchase without establishing U.S. residency or credit history.

Typical Program Parameters

  • Down payment: 25–40% typically required (vs. 20% for U.S. residents)
  • No U.S. credit history required — foreign credit reports or reference letters accepted
  • Income documented via foreign bank statements, CPA letters, or asset depletion
  • FIRPTA withholding and ITIN requirements must be navigated at closing

Asset-Based Lending

Asset Depletion & Pledged Asset Mortgages

Miami's heavy concentration of self-employed founders, private equity principals, and international investors — many of whom earn minimal W-2 income despite significant net worth — has made asset-based mortgage products a primary financing mechanism. These programs allow borrowers to qualify based on the value of liquid assets rather than documented income streams.

Asset Depletion Mechanics

  • Eligible assets divided by loan term (months) to generate a qualifying "income"
  • Brokerage, retirement, and bank accounts typically eligible (with haircuts on equities)
  • Pledged asset mortgages: pledge securities as collateral; avoid selling appreciated positions
  • Offered by Schwab Bank, Goldman Sachs, and private banking arms of major institutions

Florida Domicile & Tax Strategy

The borrowing decision and the domicile decision in Miami are inseparable for high-income earners relocating from the Northeast. Both need to be structured together — and in the right sequence.

Day Counting & Tie-Severing: The NY/NJ Audit Defense

New York's Department of Taxation is the most aggressive state revenue authority in the country for domicile audits. New York's "statutory residency" rule taxes all income if you maintain a "permanent place of abode" in New York AND spend more than 183 days in-state — regardless of where you claim domicile. For Miami purchasers who retain a Manhattan or Greenwich property, careful day management is essential from closing day forward.

Day Count Protocol

  • Maintain contemporaneous daily log (calendar, receipts, phone records)
  • Stay ≤182 days in New York if retaining any NY permanent place of abode
  • Midnight rule: any part of a day in NY counts as a full NY day
  • Document Florida presence: gym, restaurants, local medical appointments

Tie-Severing Checklist

  • Florida driver's license and vehicle registration on Day 1
  • File Florida Declaration of Domicile with the county clerk
  • Re-execute will, trusts, and POAs under Florida law
  • Notify prior state via final part-year return; register to vote in Florida

Florida Unlimited Homestead Protection

Article X, Section 4 of the Florida Constitution provides unlimited value protection for a homeowner's primary residence from forced sale by general creditors. A $20M Star Island estate receives the same constitutional protection as a modest Hialeah home — a powerful and unique asset shield for high-net-worth borrowers with business liability exposure.

  • Unlimited value protection — no dollar cap
  • Constitutional protection — cannot be amended by statute
  • Survives bankruptcy proceedings for qualifying Florida residents
  • Save Our Homes cap: assessed value growth limited to 3%/yr for homestead
  • SOH benefit is portable to a new Florida primary residence upon move

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from high-net-worth and international borrowers navigating the Miami loan market. Educational overviews only — not legal or financial advice.

What makes Miami jumbo mortgages different from jumbo loans in other U.S. markets?

Miami's jumbo market is fundamentally driven by portfolio lenders — banks and private credit institutions that hold loans on their own balance sheets rather than selling them into the secondary market. This structural difference means underwriting is more subjective, relationship-dependent, and focused on total wealth rather than income ratios alone.

The relationship premium is real: Borrowers who maintain significant deposits or investment accounts at the lending institution routinely receive 25–50 basis point rate improvements over walk-in borrowers, faster approvals, and higher LTV allowances. For a $10M purchase, 40 bps on a $7M loan represents $28,000 per year in interest savings — a meaningful return on the effort of consolidating a banking relationship.

Documentation complexity is higher: Miami's borrower base frequently presents complex income profiles — private equity distributions, carried interest, RSU vesting schedules, and international income. Portfolio lenders here have developed underwriting frameworks for these income types that most national lenders lack. Finding the right lender match for your specific income structure is often more important than rate shopping across similar lenders.

Super-jumbo transactions ($15M+) typically require extended underwriting timelines of 45–90 days and independent appraisals from MAI-certified appraisers with waterfront experience. Planning the financing timeline before making an offer is essential at this tier. This is general educational context — consult a licensed mortgage professional for current availability and terms.

Can a foreign national with no U.S. credit history finance a Miami property?

Yes — Miami is one of the few U.S. markets where foreign national mortgage financing is a mainstream, well-developed product rather than a specialty exception. Several lenders have established dedicated foreign national programs with experienced teams who manage the documentation, ITIN, and FIRPTA requirements involved.

What replaces U.S. credit history: Foreign national lenders in Miami accept foreign bank reference letters, international credit bureau reports, and in some cases will rely on the overall financial profile — bank statements, asset documentation, and employment letters — without requiring any credit history at all.

Key structural differences vs. resident borrower loans: Down payments of 25–40% are standard. Rates will be 50–150 basis points above equivalent domestic jumbo rates. Loan amounts available to foreign nationals vary significantly by lender — some cap at $2M, others will go to $10M+ for well-qualified borrowers with substantial assets on deposit.

FIRPTA: when a foreign national sells U.S. real property, FIRPTA requires the buyer to withhold 15% of the gross sale price for IRS remittance. This is a selling consideration — not a purchase barrier — but it affects total return calculations and should be factored into the purchase economics. Consult a mortgage professional and international tax attorney before proceeding. Educational overview only.

How do I qualify for a Miami jumbo mortgage if I'm self-employed with low W-2 income?

This is one of the most common situations in Miami's HNW borrower market. Founders, private equity principals, and business owners who take minimal salary but have significant net worth encounter this challenge regularly. Miami's non-QM and portfolio lending market has developed multiple alternative qualification pathways.

Bank Statement Loans: Rather than tax returns (which often show low income after business deductions), bank statement programs use 12–24 months of personal or business bank statements to calculate qualifying income. Personal accounts typically use 100% of deposits; business accounts apply a 50% expense factor.

Asset Depletion: Liquid assets are divided by the remaining loan term in months to generate a qualifying income figure. A borrower with $8M in liquid assets applying for a 30-year loan could potentially generate $22,222/month in qualifying income ($8M ÷ 360) — sufficient to qualify for a substantial loan amount.

Pledged Asset Mortgage: Offered by private banking arms of major institutions, pledged asset mortgages allow you to pledge a securities portfolio as collateral for the mortgage rather than making a traditional down payment or showing income. You maintain investment positions, avoid triggering capital gains, and often receive competitive rates in exchange for the pledged collateral. Engage a mortgage broker with deep Miami non-QM experience to evaluate your specific situation. Educational overview only.
