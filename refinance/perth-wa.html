Perth is the epicentre of Australia’s mining and resources sector, with a booming economy driven by iron ore, gold, and LNG. The mortgage market here serves FIFO workers, mining executives, engineers, and a growing professional class. Why Perth’s Mortgage Market is Australia’s Resources Powerhouse

Perth’s economy is dominated by the mining and energy sectors, creating a borrower base with high base salaries, generous allowances, and significant bonuses. FIFO (fly‑in‑fly‑out) workers are a key demographic, and lenders have adapted with specific policies for resources income. The city’s professional class (engineers, geologists, accountants) also fuels demand for prestige properties along the Swan River and coast. Western Australia’s stamp duty is lower than eastern states, but foreign investors face a 7% surcharge.

FIFO & Mining Income

Fly‑in‑fly‑out workers are a cornerstone of Perth’s economy. Lenders typically require a history of continuous employment and may average site allowances and bonuses over 2‑3 years. Some banks have specialist resources teams that understand rosters, retention payments, and even share schemes. A letter from your employer confirming ongoing work is essential.

Engineering & Professional Services

Perth is home to a large engineering workforce supporting the resources sector. Many are employed by major consultancies or work as contractors. Lenders accept contract income with 2‑3 years of history. Day‑rate mortgages are available for contractors with strong track records and current contracts.

Prestige & Riverfront Property

Peppermint Grove, Dalkeith, and Cottesloe are among Australia’s most expensive postcodes. High‑end properties often require jumbo loans (above $2M) with stricter underwriting. Lenders may require 30% deposits and extensive income verification. Interest‑only options are available for high‑net‑worth borrowers.

“Perth’s property market moves with the mining cycle. Lenders here are used to volatile incomes, but they need to see a strong track record and a healthy buffer.”

Perth Luxury Enclaves & Financing Nuances

  • Peppermint Grove / Dalkeith: $2.5M–$10M+, prestige riverside estates. Strong demand from mining executives. Jumbo loans require 30% deposits and full income documentation.
  • Cottesloe / City Beach: $1.8M–$4M, beachfront properties. High demand, often selling quickly. Lenders require valuations to match offer prices.
  • Applecross / Mount Pleasant: $1.2M–$2.5M, riverfront suburbs with family homes. Popular with professionals – pre‑approval essential for auctions.

Stamp Duty & First Home Buyer Benefits

In Western Australia, stamp duty for a $750,000 home is approximately $27,000. First‑time buyers may be eligible for a full concession on properties up to $430,000, and a tapered concession up to $530,000. The First Home Owner Grant (FHOG) offers $10,000 for new builds (house and land packages) up to $750,000. For foreign buyers, a surcharge of 7% applies on top of standard rates, making investment significantly more expensive.

Strategic insight: First‑time buyers should target new builds under $750k to access the FHOG and potentially avoid stamp duty.

Land Tax for Investors

Investors in Western Australia pay land tax on investment properties above the threshold ($300,000 for individuals, $300,000 for companies/trusts in 2026). Rates are progressive, up to 1.5% for high‑value holdings. Owner‑occupiers are exempt. This annual cost must be factored into cash flow. Some investors hold properties in trusts to manage land tax exposure, but trust structures may affect borrowing capacity.

Perth Frequently Asked Questions

How do lenders treat FIFO income in Perth?
Most lenders accept FIFO income with a history of continuous employment. They will average base salary, site allowances, and bonuses over 2‑3 years. A letter from your employer confirming ongoing work can help. Some banks have specialist resources teams and may offer higher borrowing multiples for stable FIFO workers.
What is the First Home Owner Grant in Western Australia?
The FHOG in WA is $10,000 for first‑time buyers purchasing or building a new home valued up to $750,000. It is not available for established homes. You must be an Australian citizen or permanent resident, and have never owned a property before. The grant helps with the deposit but is paid at settlement.
Is Perth a good place for property investment?
Yes, Perth offers attractive rental yields (around 4‑5%) and has historically followed the mining cycle. Current population growth and infrastructure spending are boosting demand. Investors must factor in land tax (above $300,000 threshold) and stamp duty costs. Foreign investors face a 7% surcharge. Limited company or trust ownership can be tax‑efficient but may affect borrowing.
What documents do I need if I'm a contractor in the resources sector?
Typically, you’ll need 2‑3 years of tax returns and contracts. Some lenders accept a “day rate” mortgage based on your current contract daily rate multiplied by 46 weeks. A letter from your agency or employer confirming ongoing work is essential. For “low doc” loans, you may need an accountant’s declaration and a larger deposit.
