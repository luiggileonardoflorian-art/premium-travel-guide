Why London’s Mortgage Market is Built on Stability

London’s economy is one of the most stable in Ontario, driven by insurance headquarters, post‑secondary education, and healthcare. The presence of Canada Life (London Life) and other insurers creates a deep pool of well‑compensated professionals with defined benefit pensions. Western University and Fanshawe College provide steady academic employment, while the health sciences centre is a major employer. This stability translates into favourable mortgage underwriting, though Ontario’s high land transfer tax and income tax require careful planning.

Insurance Sector & Pension Strength

London is home to Canada Life’s headquarters and numerous insurance firms. Employees enjoy defined benefit pensions that lenders view favourably. For active workers, permanent employment allows higher total debt service ratios. Retirees can often qualify with pension income grossed up by 10–15%. Many insurers also offer internal mortgage programs for employees.

Academic & Research Income

Western University employs thousands of faculty and staff. Tenured professors enjoy exceptional job security, and contract faculty with multi‑year appointments can qualify with appropriate documentation. Research grants and stipends are treated as variable income, requiring a two‑year history. The university’s pension plan is also highly regarded.

Healthcare & Hospital Employment

London Health Sciences Centre and St. Joseph’s are among the city’s largest employers. Physicians, specialists, and administrators have stable, high incomes. For doctors with incorporation, lenders may require two years of corporate financial statements. Many credit unions offer specialized products for medical professionals.

“London’s trifecta of insurance, education, and healthcare creates a borrower profile that lenders love: stable, well‑pensioned, and recession‑resistant.”

London Luxury Enclaves & Financing Nuances

  • Old North / Wortley Village: $900k–$2M, heritage homes near downtown and Western. Character properties may require additional insurance and renovation escrows. Jumbo loans available with 20% down.
  • Masonville / Sherwood Forest: $800k–$1.8M, executive homes close to Western and University Hospital. Newer construction, straightforward financing. Popular with faculty and medical professionals.
  • Oakridge / Byron: $700k–$1.4M, family‑friendly suburbs with larger lots. Lenders favour these areas for stable values and low default risk.

Ontario’s Tax Landscape & Mortgage Math

Ontario’s top marginal tax rate (combined federal & provincial) is 53.53% on income over $220,000. For high‑earning insurance executives and senior academics, this reduces after‑tax cash flow. Lenders factor this into total debt service calculations. However, stable pensions and job security can offset higher tax burdens. Some lenders offer “pension gross‑ups” of 10–15% for retired public sector and insurance employees.

Strategic insight: Maximize RRSP contributions to lower taxable income and increase after‑tax cash flow — lenders consider RRSP room as a positive factor.

Land Transfer Tax: A Major Closing Cost

London buyers pay the Ontario land transfer tax: 0.5% on first $55k, 1% on $55k–$250k, 1.5% on $250k–$400k, and 2% on amounts over $400k. On a $1M home, that’s approximately $16,475. First‑time buyers may qualify for a rebate up to $4,000. This upfront cost must be factored into your down payment and closing cash.

London Frequently Asked Questions

How do lenders treat income from insurance companies like Canada Life?
Insurance employment is considered highly stable. Lenders use base salary as full qualifying income. Bonuses and commissions are averaged over two years. Defined benefit pensions (common in insurance) are often grossed up by 10–15% for retired borrowers. Some insurers have dedicated mortgage programs for employees through affiliated lenders.
Can university faculty with contract appointments qualify for a mortgage?
Yes, if you have a two‑year history of contract renewals and a current contract extending at least six months. Tenured faculty have the easiest path; sessional instructors may need a larger down payment. Research grants can be counted if consistently received and shown on tax returns.
What is the land transfer tax rebate for first‑time buyers in Ontario?
First‑time buyers in Ontario can receive a rebate of up to $4,000 on the provincial land transfer tax. To qualify, you must be at least 18, a Canadian citizen or permanent resident, and have never owned a home anywhere in the world. The rebate is applied at closing, reducing the cash needed.
Are there foreign buyer restrictions or taxes in London?
The federal Prohibition on the Purchase of Residential Property by Non‑Canadians Act applies across Canada, including London. Exemptions exist for work permit holders with sufficient tenure, international students (with conditions), and diplomats. Ontario’s Non‑Resident Speculation Tax (NRST) does NOT apply in London — it only applies in the Greater Golden Horseshoe region. Foreign nationals who qualify for an exemption may need 35%+ down.
