Why El Paso's Mortgage Market Is Distinct

El Paso's economy is anchored by Fort Bliss (one of the largest military installations in the U.S.), a robust healthcare sector, and a growing manufacturing and logistics base. The housing market offers exceptional affordability, a mix of established neighborhoods and new construction, and a significant military population. Mortgage strategies here must account for VA loan benefits, physician lending, and Texas's property tax structure.

Military & VA Lending

Fort Bliss drives a massive military population. VA loans offer 0% down, no PMI, and competitive rates. Lenders in El Paso are highly experienced with VA requirements, including BAH as income, funding fee financing, and disabled veteran exemptions. Many service members also qualify for USDA loans in outlying areas.

Healthcare & Physician Lending

University Medical Center, Las Palmas, and Sierra Providence employ thousands of medical professionals. Physician mortgage loans — offering 0‑5% down, no PMI — are widely available for doctors, residents, and advanced practitioners. These loans often exclude student loan debt from DTI calculations, making homeownership accessible earlier in a medical career.

New Construction & Affordability

El Paso's housing market remains one of the most affordable in Texas, with significant new construction in the East Side, West Side, and Far East areas. Construction‑to‑permanent loans (one‑time close) are popular. Many builders offer incentives, but buyers should compare against independent lender options.

Neighborhood Nuances & Financing

  • Kern Place / Sunset Heights: Historic homes near UTEP, renovation loans (203k) common. Strong demand from faculty and professionals.
  • Coronado Hills / Westside: Luxury homes, excellent schools. Jumbo loans with larger lots; some with private wells.
  • East Side / Far East: New construction, master‑planned communities. Construction‑perm loans and builder incentives.
  • Cielo Vista / Mission Hills: Established suburbs, good access to base and medical centers. Conventional and VA loans prevalent.

Texas Tax Advantages

Texas has no personal income tax — a major advantage for military, healthcare professionals, and all wage earners. However, property taxes are among the highest in the nation, averaging 2.1% in El Paso County. For mortgage qualification, the absence of state income tax boosts net income and DTI capacity. But property taxes must be escrowed and can be a significant monthly cost. Texas also has homestead exemptions that reduce taxable value for primary residences.

Strategic insight: The lack of income tax means your mortgage payment capacity may be higher than in income‑tax states. However, factor in the full property tax burden and higher homeowners insurance due to severe weather risk (hail, wind).

Comparison with Neighboring States

Compared to New Mexico (income tax up to 5.9%) and Arizona (up to 4.5%), Texas's lack of income tax is a significant draw. However, property taxes in Texas are generally higher. For cross‑border workers commuting from Ciudad Juárez, tax residency rules become complex — consult a tax professional. Texas also has no estate tax and strong homestead protections.

El Paso Frequently Asked Questions

I'm active duty at Fort Bliss. How do I qualify for a VA loan?
You'll need a Certificate of Eligibility (COE), which your lender can help obtain. VA loans offer 0% down, no PMI, and competitive rates. Your Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) counts as income. Funding fee can be financed into the loan, and may be waived if you're a disabled veteran. Many local lenders specialize in VA loans for Fort Bliss personnel.
I'm a physician at University Medical Center. Can I get a mortgage with no down payment?
Yes. Several lenders offer physician loans that require 0% down for residents, fellows, and attending physicians. These programs often exclude student loan debt from DTI calculations and use your future earning potential. Some require 5% down for attendings. Check with local banks and national lenders specializing in medical professionals.
What are the property tax rates in El Paso County?
El Paso County's effective property tax rate averages about 2.1% of assessed value, which includes city, county, school district, and special district taxes. Texas has no state property tax. Homestead exemptions can reduce taxable value for owner‑occupants. Be sure to factor property taxes into your monthly payment, as they are typically escrowed.
Are there first‑time homebuyer programs in El Paso?
Yes. The Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation (TSAHC) offers down payment assistance programs for first‑time buyers. The City of El Paso also has programs through the Community Development Department. USDA loans are available in many outlying areas with 0% down. Income limits apply. Many local lenders are approved for these programs.
