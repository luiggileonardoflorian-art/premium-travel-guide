Why Sydney’s Mortgage Market is Australia’s Most Complex

Sydney’s economy is anchored by banking, professional services, and a booming tech sector. This creates a borrower base with diverse income: bonuses, share options, and self‑employed earnings. The city’s astronomical property prices mean high loan amounts and strict serviceability buffers (3% above the loan rate). Investors face additional hurdles with land tax and foreign surcharges, while first‑home buyers rely on stamp duty concessions and government schemes.

Banking & Bonus Income

Sydney is home to the headquarters of Australia’s Big Four banks. Bonuses and commissions form a significant part of compensation. Lenders typically average bonus income over 2‑3 years and require a letter from your employer confirming ongoing eligibility. Some banks have dedicated teams for financial services professionals.

Tech & Share Options

Sydney’s tech scene (Atlassian, Canva, countless startups) means many employees receive share options or RSUs. Lenders may consider vested options as a liquid asset, and some specialist lenders can use a portion of unexercised options to boost borrowing capacity. A two‑year history of exercising options is ideal.

Self‑Employed & SME Owners

Sydney’s economy is fuelled by small business owners. Lenders typically require 2‑3 years of tax returns and may use “low doc” loans (with a higher rate) for those with one year’s accounts. Some specialist lenders offer loans based on business cash flow rather than just net profit.

Sydney Luxury Enclaves & Financing Nuances

  • Eastern Suburbs (Vaucluse, Double Bay): $3M–$20M+, prestige waterfront. Jumbo loans often require 30%+ deposits. Lenders scrutinise foreign income carefully.
  • North Shore (Mosman, Neutral Bay): $2M–$8M, family homes with water views. High demand, often selling at auction. Lenders require valuation to match contract price.
  • Inner West (Balmain, Annandale): $1.5M–$4M, character homes and terraces. Many heritage‑listed — renovation loans may require council approvals.

Stamp Duty & First Home Buyer Benefits

In NSW, stamp duty for a $1.5M home is approximately $67,000. First‑time buyers may be eligible for a full exemption on properties up to $650,000 or a concession up to $800,000. The First Home Buyer Choice allows you to opt for an annual property tax instead of upfront stamp duty. For foreign buyers, a surcharge of 8% applies on top of standard rates, making investment significantly more expensive.

Strategic insight: For first‑time buyers, the annual property tax option can free up cash for a larger deposit.

Land Tax for Investors

Investors in NSW pay land tax on investment properties above the threshold ($1,075,000 in 2026, with a premium rate of 1.6% above $2,366,000). Owner‑occupiers are exempt. This annual cost must be factored into cash flow. Some investors hold properties in trusts to manage land tax exposure, but trust structures may affect borrowing capacity.

Sydney Frequently Asked Questions

How do lenders assess bonus income for bankers in Sydney?
Most lenders will average your bonus over the last 2‑3 years. If your bonus has increased significantly, they may use the most recent year if you provide a letter from your employer confirming it’s likely to continue. Some banks have dedicated teams for financial services professionals who understand bonus structures.
Can I use share options from my tech employer to qualify?
Yes, some specialist lenders accept vested share options as a liquid asset. A two‑year history of exercising options is ideal. For unexercised options, lenders may consider a percentage of the in‑the‑money value as a deposit boost. You’ll need option grant statements and possibly a letter from your employer.
What is the First Home Buyer Choice in NSW?
First‑time buyers in NSW can choose to pay an annual property tax instead of upfront stamp duty. This option is available for properties up to $1.5 million. It can significantly reduce the initial cash needed, but you’ll pay an ongoing land tax‑style charge. It’s worth comparing the long‑term cost with a mortgage broker.
Are there restrictions for foreign buyers in Sydney?
Yes, foreign buyers need FIRB approval (Foreign Investment Review Board) and pay a surcharge of 8% on top of standard stamp duty. They are generally restricted to new dwellings or off‑plan purchases. Lenders may require higher deposits (often 30‑40%) and may have higher rates. It’s essential to consult a specialist cross‑border broker.
